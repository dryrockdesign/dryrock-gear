Inside the Spur Gear Designer

What it actually calculates

This isn't a shape generator that just draws a gear. It runs the real polymer-gear engineering checks from the VDI 2736 standard, so the part you print is one you can actually trust under load.

Gear geometry

Define a true involute gear from the parameters that matter:

  • Module, number of teeth, pressure angle
  • Profile shift (x) for stronger or fitted gears
  • Root fillet radius & tip/root diameters
  • Single gear or a meshing pair with ratio

Strength & safety (VDI 2736)

The core engineering verification for plastic gears:

  • Tooth-root bending stress (σF)
  • Flank / contact pressure (σH, Hertzian)
  • Safety factors SF and SH
  • Pass / fail verdict for your load case

Thermal & wear check

Plastic gears fail from heat, not just force:

  • Flash & bulk tooth temperature estimate
  • PV (pressure × velocity) loading
  • Indicative wear & service-life guidance
  • Warns when a material runs too hot

Material library

Pick your real printing filament — properties are built in:

  • POM, PA66, PA12, PA-CF, PETG, ABS, PLA, PC, TPU
  • Custom material option for your own data
  • Print-strength derating for FDM parts

Hub, bore & fit

Make it a real, mountable part:

  • Round bore, D-flat, keyway (DIN 6885-1) or hex
  • Hub diameter & length
  • Clearance / fit allowance for printed parts

Exports you can use

Take your verified design straight to the bench:

  • Print-ready STL (generated on our server)
  • Full PDF engineering report of every result
  • Save / load your design as a config file

Also included

Live 3D preview & mesh animation
Working pressure angle & centre distance
Contact ratio calculation
Pair kinematics: torque, speed, efficiency
Profile-shift optimiser (balances root safety)
Basic & Advanced input modes

Core gear geometry & VDI 2736 stress results are calculated precisely. Thermal, PV and wear figures are indicative guidance — for critical or load-bearing parts, confirm with physical testing.
